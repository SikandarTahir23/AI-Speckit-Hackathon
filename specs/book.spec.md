# Hackathon Specification: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook

## Goal
Generate a comprehensive, university-level textbook using Docusaurus format for a course on Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics. The content should be suitable for engineering students and include concepts, math, and practical examples.

## Output Format
The output should be Docusaurus Markdown (`.md`) files placed in the `docs` directory, following the specified syllabus structure.

## Updated Syllabus (Table of Contents)

1. **Introduction to Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics**
    - 1.1 The Partnership of People, AI Agents, and Robots  
    - 1.2 History and Evolution of Humanoid Designs  
    - 1.3 Key Components: Actuators, Sensors, and Power Systems  

2. **Robot Kinematics and Dynamics**
    - 2.1 Forward and Inverse Kinematics (Detailed mathematical derivations)  
    - 2.2 Jacobian and Manipulator Dynamics  
    - 2.3 Motion Planning and Trajectory Generation  

3. **Sensing and Perception**
    - 3.1 Vision Systems (Camera processing, Lidar)  
    - 3.2 Haptic and Force Sensing  
    - 3.3 Sensor Fusion Techniques  

4. **Vision-Language-Action (VLA) Systems**
    - 4.1 VLA Model Architecture: Inputs, Outputs, and Multimodal Learning  
    - 4.2 Vision Encoder, Language Encoder, Fusion Module, Action Decoder  
    - 4.3 VLA Architectures: RT-1, RT-2, OpenVLA  
    - 4.4 Training Approaches: Imitation Learning, Reinforcement Learning, Pre-training + Fine-tuning  
    - 4.5 Practical Applications: Manipulation with RT-1, Multi-step Task Execution, Integration Pipelines  
    - 4.6 Challenges: Data Requirements, Sim-to-Real Gap, Safety, Computational Costs  

5. **Capstone Project: Simple AI-Robot Pipeline**
    - 5.1 Designing an End-to-End AI-Robot Pipeline  
    - 5.2 Layered Architecture: Perception → Cognition → Planning → Control  
    - 5.3 Practical Implementation in ROS 2: Object Dete
